What is CE-5?

Quiet, intentional contact, not the search for lights in the sky.

CE5 stands for Close Encounter of the Fifth Kind. It is a peaceful practice of human initiated contact, where a group gathers under the night sky in meditation, stillness, and shared intention. Together, we extend a calm and respectful invitation to any non-human intelligence that may be present.

Read FAQs

Meditative

At its heart, CE-5 is a meditative group field practice. We sit together in the dark, settle the mind, and hold a calm, coherent, welcoming state. No equipment required, only presence, patience, and respect.

Opportunities for subtle contact

Contact may occur in ways that are subtle and easy to dismiss. For most encounters there is always the option to believe that something is happening or its just your imagination. But its through synchronicity and imagination that a great deal of our contact occurs. Our imagination is the doorway into the higher self and to contact.

Service

Contact should always be done as a form of service. In performing the puja before CE5 we request that the contact be of a nature that promotes our own spiritual growth. In this way, it is for the highest good.

The contact protocols we follow were developed by Dr. Steven Greer and CSETI, the Center for the Study of Extraterrestrial Intelligence. We are an independent group practicing in that tradition.

Our flagship gathering of the season, timed to the darkest skies of the lunar month. Newcomers are warmly welcome — most of the night is gentle, guided, and unhurried.

We begin at dusk with camp setup and a shared meal, then move into an orientation that covers what CE-5 is, what to expect, and how we keep the field calm and coherent. After a guided meditation we head out to the observation clearing together.

There is no pressure to "see" anything. The practice itself — sitting quietly under the stars with a welcoming group — is the point. We stay out until the early hours, taking breaks at the fire as needed.

Evening schedule

6:30 PM Orientation for first-timers or beginners
7:30 PM Shared meal at the fire ring
8:30 PM Puja & guided meditation
9:30 PM Move to the observation clearing
1:30 AM Wind down · overnight camp

Getting there

The retreat is about a 30-minute drive on gravel forest roads from the nearest highway. Exact coordinates and a meeting point are emailed to registered attendees 48 hours before the event. High-clearance vehicles help in wet weather but are not required in dry conditions.

Weather & Conditions

Check the weather below before arriving, as fires nearby can create smoke that will prevent a CE5 from having clear skies.

Clear skies forecast with a low near 48°F. Light breeze and excellent visibility through the new-moon window.
Air quality good (AQI 22) — no active wildfire smoke in the area.
What to bring
Sleeping bag, pad & tent
Warm layers (nights drop near 45°F)
Red-light flashlight or headlamp
Folding chair or reclining mat
Water & a mug for tea
Food to share (optional)
Special notes

Beginner-friendly. We meet at dusk for orientation and a guided meditation before fieldwork. Location is 40 minutes from a hotel; camping is available on-site.

We pair our field practice with the peak of the Perseid meteor shower — one of the best nights of the year to simply lie back and watch the sky.
