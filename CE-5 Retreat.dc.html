What is CE-5?

Quiet, intentional, safe contact, not the search for lights in the sky.

CE-5 stands for Close Encounter of the Fifth Kind. It is a peaceful practice of human initiated contact, where a group gathers under the night sky in meditation, stillness, and shared intention. Together, we extend a calm and respectful invitation to any non-human intelligence that may be present.  We do so under the protection of a puja ceremony as has been done by some cultures for thousands of years.

Read FAQs

Meditative

At its heart, CE-5 is a meditative group field practice. We sit together in the dark, settle the mind, and hold a calm, coherent, welcoming state. No equipment required, only presence, patience, and respect.

Opportunities for subtle contact

Contact may occur in ways that are subtle and easy to dismiss. For most encounters there is always the option to believe that something is happening or its just your imagination. But its through synchronicity and imagination that a great deal of our contact occurs. Our imagination is the doorway into the higher self and to contact.

Service

Contact should always be done as a form of service. In performing the puja before CE-5 we request that the contact be of a nature that promotes our own spiritual growth. In this way, it is for the highest good.

The contact protocols we follow were developed by Dr. Steven Greer and CSETI, the Center for the Study of Extraterrestrial Intelligence. We are an independent group practicing in that tradition.

Our first gathering of the season takes place beneath some of the darkest skies in Washington. Join us for a peaceful evening of meditation, curiosity, and shared wonder.

This gathering is limited to 20 people, so early registration is recommended.

Please arrive about 7pm which will give ample time to setup and to have an introduction to CE5 to learn what the evening will involve, and how to participate comfortably.

Getting there

The Experiencer Center is located 30 minutes from Kettle Falls, Washington. Specific location details will be given after registration.

Camping

Camping on-site is the heart of the experience. Stay out under some of Washington's darkest skies and wake up in the quiet of the forest. Pitch a tent in the clearing and settle in for the full night. And you won't be roughing it the next morning: the center is well equipped with comfortable amenities to help you ease into the day. Campers must be checked out by 11 am the following morning.

Outdoor shower with hot water
Outdoor kitchen with a fridge, microwave, coffee maker, electric water kettle, grill, and toaster
