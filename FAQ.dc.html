Tips & Answers

What to know

We love doing CE-5 and look forward to spending time out under the stars with you. Here are some specific tips to help you have the very best experience, followed by general FAQs below.

Before you come · Top tips
01

Practice the contact protocols the week before

To deepen your contact experience, spend the week before the event engaging with the contact protocols. Many participants report this preparation greatly enhanced what they experienced, with some encountering beautiful contact before the gathering even began. Each day, practice a brief 5 to 10 minute meditation paired with a coherent thought sequence (CTS). We'll send detailed CTS instructions in materials after your register.

02

Bring a comfortable chair

Don't forget to bring a chair. Most people use zero-gravity recliners — they're far comfier for meditating and watching the stars (see the FAQs below for more).

03

Bring a water canteen

Invest in a reusable water canteen. We provide cool, filtered drinking water on site, so a refillable container keeps you hydrated and helps us avoid plastic waste.

04

Plan for mosquitoes in summer

During the summer months, bring mosquito protection — either chemical repellent or clothing that covers your arms, legs, and ankles. Some people pick up inexpensive netting online.

05

No experience needed — just relax

Don't worry at all if you don't feel ready. This is an introduction, and there will be many first-time attendees with little to no experience. Just relax and come enjoy the evening with us.

The basics

Frequently asked questions

CE-5 stands for Close Encounter of the Fifth Kind. It's a peaceful practice of human-initiated contact, where a group gathers under the night sky in meditation, stillness, and shared intention. Together we extend a calm and respectful invitation to any non-human intelligence that may be present. At its heart it's a meditative group field practice — no equipment required, only presence, patience, and respect.

None at all. All levels are welcome. You don't need any prior experience, special equipment, or particular beliefs — only curiosity and an open, respectful mind. Beginners should attend the orientation session, which gives an overview, instruction, and a chance to ask questions before the practice begins.

Plan to arrive around 7pm, which gives ample time to set up and take part in an introduction to CE-5 — what the evening involves and how to participate comfortably. The Puja begins at 9:00, and all participants should be present for it. From there we settle into the clearing for guided meditation and sky-watching, remaining into the early morning with the freedom to rest or take breaks whenever you need.

An important part of CE-5 is that it's done without expectation, but in gratitude. There's no pressure to "see" anything — the heart of the experience is simply being together beneath the stars, quiet, present, and open to possibility. Contact, when it comes, is often subtle and easy to dismiss, arriving through synchronicity and imagination as much as anything in the sky.
What to bring

You will need a chair to sit in. Although anything will do, the foldable sport chairs are popular. Most people who do CE-5 often invest in a zero-gravity recliner camping chair — they cost a little more but are much comfier for meditating and watching the stars.

Bringing a red flashlight is ideal. Exposure to bright white light will impair your night vision and that of those around you for a good 20–30 minutes. You may use your white lights before or after the event. Please avoid the use of any head-mounted lights in the circle to avoid blinding other attendees (head lights are OK for photographers outside the circle).

On the day of the event you'll be more comfortable eating lightly. Eating a big, heavy meal could make you uncomfortable during the meditations. Having a few healthy snacks available will make the evening more pleasant.

We provide several large water coolers with filtered drinking water on site. Please bring a water canteen or jug to help us avoid creating lots of plastic waste.

Summer evenings bring mosquitoes — and ants too. We strongly recommend appropriate clothing: socks and shoes that cover the lower leg, long pants, and a long-sleeve windbreaker to protect your arms and torso. A hat with mosquito netting (available online) is also not a bad idea, as it keeps the buzzing away from your ears that can sometimes make meditation difficult.

We'll be surrounded by trees, grass, and plants of all kinds. If you have seasonal allergies, we suggest taking your favorite medication before attending.
Rules & safety

No, no, and no. Even caffeine can make meditation more challenging. Over years of working with experiencers, we've found that using drugs and alcohol during a contact modality can lead to dangerous outcomes. If you are found using or in possession of these, you will be removed from the event.

Due to the very real and mortal risk of forest fire, we cannot allow smoking on the property.

We are very cognizant about laser safety. You may bring a laser of 100mW or less to use when invited by the coordinator — please do not use it except when invited, and then with extreme caution. Any laser over 100mW (300mW absolute max) must be approved during registration. We never point a laser at any object in the sky, and certainly never at any object on the ground. Please do not bring a laser if it is very new to you or you are not extremely familiar with it.

People have captured extraordinary photographs with just their cell phones. We encourage photography of the area before and after the event by everyone — just be mindful that some people may not wish to be photographed. We ask that only people very familiar with their equipment and/or with CE-5 take pictures during the contact event, to avoid distracting the group. All lights on any camera used during the contact portion must be taped or hidden (we have gaffer's tape on site for your use). Please, no IR illumination or flash of any type.

We cannot allow leashed or unleashed pets on the premises due to the presence of great cats and bears. In addition, pet owners have told us that concern for their pet was distracting enough to keep them from reaching the full meditative state necessary for contact work.
Cost, camping & logistics

Registration for a CE-5 is $15, with optional on-site camping for an additional $25. When registering on Eventbrite, select the correct ticket for what you need. Each event is capped at a limited number of attendees, so early registration is recommended.

Gatherings are held at The Experiencer Center, located about 30 minutes from Kettle Falls, Washington, under some of the darkest skies in the state. The exact event location is shared privately with registered attendees — specific driving instructions are emailed to you after you register.

If you wish to camp, you must bring your own camping gear. No fires of any type are allowed by the land owners. If you'd rather sleep indoors, there are a few hotels and B&Bs roughly 35 minutes away — you'll find their details on each event's page.

There are two basic event liability forms — one for the land owners and one for our CE-5 group. We make them available for download before the event and have them on site as well.

Event-specific information like the schedule and driving instructions to the contact site is emailed to you through Eventbrite. Consider adding our email address to your contacts list to help keep our emails out of your spam folder. Don't kid yourself though — they'll still end up in your spam or promotions folder, so check there anyway. :)
